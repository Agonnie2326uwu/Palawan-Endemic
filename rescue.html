Act fast, call the right number

Wildlife rescue & cruelty hotlines

If an animal is injured, in danger, being sold, or being mistreated — these are the numbers to call. Every endemic species on this site is protected by Philippine law.

Life-threatening emergency (person injured)

911

National Emergency Hotline — for serious bites, bleeding, or any injury requiring urgent medical transport. Call this first if a person is hurt.

Wildlife in distress, trapped, or being trafficked

Found an injured, trapped, sick, or illegally captured wild animal anywhere in Palawan? Contact the Palawan Council for Sustainable Development Staff (PCSDS) Wildlife Enforcement Unit directly.

PCSDS Wildlife Enforcement Unit (WEU) Hotline

0931 964 2128 (TNT)
0965 662 0248 (TM)

For rescues, injured wildlife, sightings of wildlife for sale, snares/traps, and trafficking in progress.

PCSDS Front Desk

0935 116 2336 (Globe/TM)

General PCSD office line if the WEU hotline above is unreachable.

DENR Biodiversity Management Bureau — Wildlife Crime

(02) 925 8952 / 925 8953

National DENR line for reporting wildlife trafficking anywhere in the Philippines. Also reachable via facebook.com/denrbiodiversity.

Palawan Wildlife Rescue & Conservation Center (PWRCC)

Barangay Irawan, Puerto Princesa City — the region's main rehabilitation facility for confiscated and injured wildlife, including the Philippine Crocodile breeding program. Wildlife is typically routed here through PCSDS after a call to the WEU hotline above.

Reporting animal cruelty

Animal cruelty and neglect — including against wildlife — is a criminal offense under RA 8485, the Animal Welfare Act, as amended by RA 10631, with fines up to ₱100,000 and imprisonment of up to three years. Wildlife-specific cruelty and trafficking is additionally covered under RA 9147, the Wildlife Resources Conservation and Protection Act.

If it involves a wild / endemic animal

Call the PCSDS Wildlife Enforcement Unit numbers above. This covers all 14 species in this guide and any other native Palawan wildlife.

If it involves a domestic animal

Contact your local barangay office, the Department of Agriculture's Committee on Animal Welfare, or your nearest PNP station — they handle non-wildlife cruelty cases such as pets and livestock.

What to do while you wait for a response

  • Keep a safe distance — do not attempt to free, move, or confront anyone yourself.
  • If safe to do so, note the exact location (barangay, nearby landmark, or GPS pin).
  • Photos or video help enormously, but only take them if it's safe and doesn't put you at risk.
  • Do not post exact locations of rare species publicly on social media — this can attract poachers.
