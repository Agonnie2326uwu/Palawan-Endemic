A field guide to Palawan's wildlife

Endemic life,
found nowhere else on Earth.

Palawan is home to animals that exist in no other forest, river, or reef on the planet — many found nowhere outside this one island group. This guide covers who they are, where to find them, and what to do if you meet one in the wild.

Why Palawan

An island that evolved on its own.

Separated from the rest of the Philippine archipelago for millions of years, Palawan's wildlife shares more in common with Borneo than with Luzon or Mindanao. That isolation produced a roster of animals — pangolins, peacock-pheasants, bearcats, hornbills — that live only here.

Why this guide exists

Identification, safety, and protection.

Every entry includes conservation status, what to do (and not do) if you encounter the animal, first aid if a bite or injury is genuinely possible, and how to report wildlife crime or call for a rescue. Knowing the facts protects both people and animals.
